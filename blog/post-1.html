5 Essential Steps Before Making an Offer on Your Dream Home

June 5, 2026 By Mystie Schmitz Buying Tips

Making an offer on a home is one of the biggest decisions you'll make. In the competitive Florida Panhandle real estate market, being prepared and strategic can mean the difference between landing your dream home and missing out. Here are the five essential steps you should take before submitting an offer.

1. Get Pre-Approved for a Mortgage

Before you start looking at homes, get pre-approved for a mortgage. Pre-approval shows sellers that you're a serious buyer with confirmed financing. This puts you in a stronger negotiating position and helps you understand your budget clearly. Work with a lender to determine how much you can comfortably afford, keeping in mind additional costs like property taxes, insurance, and HOA fees.

2. Understand the Local Market

Knowledge is power in real estate. Work with a local agent (like me!) to understand current market conditions in your desired area. Are prices rising or falling? How quickly are homes selling? What's the average days on market? Understanding these trends helps you make a competitive offer without overpaying or underestimating the market.

In the Florida Panhandle, market conditions can vary significantly between Crestview, Destin, Fort Walton Beach, and surrounding areas. Local expertise is invaluable.

3. Schedule a Home Inspection

Once you've identified a property you love, schedule a professional home inspection before making an offer. This inspection will reveal any structural issues, mechanical problems, or needed repairs. The inspection contingency in your offer gives you the right to negotiate repairs or request price reductions based on findings.

4. Research Property Details and History

Dig deeper into the property's background:

  • Title Search: Ensure the seller has a clear title to the property
  • Property Records: Review the property's history, previous ownership, and any liens
  • Neighborhood Research: Check out the area, nearby schools, parks, and planned developments
  • Flood Zone Verification: Especially important in Florida, confirm flood zone status and insurance costs

5. Work With an Experienced Real Estate Agent

This is perhaps the most critical step. An experienced agent will guide you through the entire process, help you craft a competitive offer, and protect your interests throughout the transaction. Your agent will:

  • Help you determine a fair offer price based on comparable sales
  • Advise on contingencies and terms that protect you
  • Negotiate on your behalf if the seller counters your offer
  • Ensure all deadlines and requirements are met
